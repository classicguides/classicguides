Priest

This guide focuses on Healing, as thats what you'll likely be doing as a priest in classic WoW.

Source: Vanilla Priest

Priests are powerful healers, and can be deadly as ranged DPS, if specced properly. Priests were long the premier healers in World of Warcraft, and although other classes have been improved to match their prowess, priests will still generally be assumed to fill the healer role in groups. Players who do not enjoy healing are thus advised to consider a different class, as playing a priest guarantees that you will be healing at some point. Priests can specialize in the shadow tree to become extremely effective single-target ranged DPS casters, and at higher levels (50+) the damage they cause can return mana to their group. Priests are also able to cast a number of very useful buffs that any party will find useful.

Priests 3 talent specializations are Holy, Discipline, and Shadow.

Consumables

Below I’ve listed some common consumables. There are others out there of course, but this should do you for the most part.

Common Consumables:
  • Major Mana Potion - MUST HAVE!
  • Demonic Rune - MUST HAVE!

Major Mana Potions should be regularly available on AH for a reasonable price or you can loot them from the chest at the end of DM North (Tribute) runs.
Demonic Runes can be farmed from northern Felwood—make friends with a DPS who’ll help you do this or multi-box if allowed.

You should stock up on these for every raid and use them early on in boss fights so that they cool down and you can use them again if needed. As you gain more exposure to raiding, you’ll get better at knowing how long fights go for and how much mana you’re going to burn.

Easy-to-Obtain Consumables
  • Crystal Force (+20 Spirit)
  • Kreeg's Stout Beatdown (+25 Spirit, -5 Intellect)
  • Lesser Mana Oil (+8 MP5)
Hard-to-Obtain Consumables (due to mats required/low availability on AH)
  • Brilliant Mana Oil (+12 MP5)
  • Elixir of the Sages (+18 Intellect and Spirit)
  • Flask of Distilled Wisdom (+2000 Mana)
  • Greater Fire/Shadow/Nature/Frost Protection Potions
  • Nightfin Soup (+9 mp5)
  • Sagefish Delight (+6 mp5)

Depending on your server, some of the above consumables may not stack with buffs (e.g. Crystal Force and Divine Spirit). Be sure to investigate before obtaining too many.


Healing Essentials


Downranking

Mastering how to downrank is key to becoming a great healer. The idea is to use lower level spells that cost less mana. These spells will still be effective because of the + healing from your gear.

In raids downranking is mandatory as there can be up to 10 healers in the group and one or more of them will be casting a heal on the same target as you. Essentially, you want to heal someone just enough and avoid overhealing them as much as possible (which is a waste of mana).

Start by experimenting with a mix of different ranked spells which heal for different amounts.

I’m currently using the following:

  • Heal Rank 3, Greater Heal Rank 1 and Flash Heal Rank 4
    - Used to top people off
  • Flash Heal Rank 7:
    - Used in high-intensity situations to stop people from dying
  • Greater Heal 4:
    - Used only on tanks when necessary
  • Renew and Prayer of Healing (max rank): - Used situationally

You should keep changing this up as you become more experienced and more geared. I personally don’t like having too many keybinds or clicking a lot, so I’m probably sticking with max rank Renew and Prayer of Healing for a long time but will keep tinkering with Heal/Greater Heal/Flash Heal.

Fade

Priests are lucky to have such a great threat drop. It has saved me countless times in Onyxia when there’s a lot of whelps chasing me or in BWL during Razorgore when there’s a loose dragon. If you’re anything like me (who came from healing as a druid before playing a priest), you might forget you have it, so practice using it as much as you can in appropriate situations.

Mana Regen While Casting

The ‘five second rule’

In the Discipline tree, there’s a talent called Meditation that allows up to “15% of mana regeneration to continue while casting.” Have you ever wondered what is meant by “while casting”? For a long time, I thought it meant while you were casting a spell. It’s actually referring to the ‘five second rule’. After you cast a spell, you’re considered ‘casting’ for the next five seconds and won’t regenerate any mana from spirit, only from mp5. Try it, cast a spell and count to five, only then will you see your mana start to regenerate in big ticks. Once you’re out of the casting state you will regenerate these ticks every two seconds.

This is important to know for a couple of reasons.

Firstly, in dungeons and especially in raids, you’re usually always in the ‘casting’ state during fights which is why it’s important to try and increase your spirit and the % of mana you regenerate while casting. Therefore Meditation is a non-negotiable talent and you should try to get the 3-set bonus from the t2 priest set (Vestments of Transcendence) ASAP which will bring you to a total of 30%.

Following this logic, in contrast, when shadow specced for PVE you should aim for mp5 over spirit as you’ll only have access to Meditation for 15% mana regeneration while casting. You won’t have the additional 15% from the t2 priest set (you wouldn’t wear it because it only provides + healing, not + damage).

The second thing is that when you’re more experienced, you can coordinate spell casts with the five second rule in mind. For example:

  • Cast Greater Heal
  • The five second timer begins
  • Wait three 3 seconds
  • Start casting your second Greater Heal
  • Five seconds pass since the first cast so you get a tick of mana back
  • Second Greater Heal finishes casting and timer starts again
  • Rinse and repeat for maximum mana efficiency

This isn’t viable all the time but works in low-intensity situations. If you want to try this, I’d recommend downloading the TNE FiveSec addon.

Power Word: Shield

As a general rule, in PVE Power Word: Shield should only be in used in emergencies and situationally depending on the fight, not as a main healing spell. Note that warriors generate most of their threat from damage taken (so refrain from shielding them unless necessary), whereas druids generate most of their threat from damage done (so it’s fine to shield them but still not recommended).

Renew

Renew is great in terms of healing per second and is not worth downranking in my opinion (though I’ve heard it can be in much later raids like Naxx). If you want to include it as a part of your dungeon rotation then you can, especially on the tank. In raids, there can only be 1 Renew on a target at any time so unless you’re the assigned tank Renew priest (which should be the one with the highest + healing), you don’t need necessarily need to spend talent points on Improved Renew.

Here’s a macro you can copy paste which will ensure you don’t overwrite an existing Renew:

/run m=0 for i=1,40 do if(strfind(tostring(UnitBuff(“target”,i)),”Spell_Holy_Renew”))then m=1 end end if m==0 then CastSpellByName(“Renew(Rank 9)”) end

Top up the tank in between pulls

It’s important to top the tank up in between pulls. Otherwise, on the next pull you’ll need to cast a big heal on the tank before he’s had a chance to build threat on multiple targets which means they’ll come straight for you.

Healing

    - These will come naturally with gear you obtain for pre-BiS/BiS.
